UNIT 1 – OUTBREAK OF WORLD WAR I & AFTERMATH

1914 – Beginning of First World War

First Industrial War: World War I

Empires Destroyed

  • Germany
  • Austria–Hungary
  • Ottoman Empire

Causes of World War I

1. Imperialism: Defined by Lenin as the “Highest stage of capitalism.”

2. Alliances:

Triple Alliance (1882): Germany, Austria–Hungary, Italy

Triple Entente (1907): Britain, France, Russia

3. Nationalism:

  • England – Jingoism
  • France – Chauvinism
  • Germany – Kultur

4. Balkan Crisis: “Powder Keg of Europe”

Immediate Cause (28 June 1914)

Archduke Franz Ferdinand assassinated at Sarajevo by Princip (Bosnian Serb)

Course of the War

Central Powers: Germany, Austria–Hungary, Turkey, Bulgaria

Allies: Britain, France, Russia, Italy, USA, Belgium, Serbia, Romania, Greece

Important Battles

  • Tannenberg – Russia defeated
  • Marne – Beginning of trench warfare
  • Verdun – Five months battle
  • Somme – Heavy British loss
  • Jutland – Naval battle

Lusitania (1917): American ship sunk → USA entered the war

Treaty of Versailles (28 June 1919)

Signed in the Hall of Mirrors

Big Three:

  • Woodrow Wilson (USA)
  • Lloyd George (UK)
  • Clemenceau (France)

Rhineland: Demilitarised

Russian Revolution (1917)

Bloody Sunday (1905): Led by Father Gapon

March Revolution: Tsar Nicholas II abdicated

November Revolution: Lenin seized power

Pravda: Means “Truth”

Treaty of Brest-Litovsk (March 1918): Russia withdrew from WWI after signing with Germany

League of Nations (1920)

Founder: Woodrow Wilson

Headquarters: Geneva, Switzerland

First Secretary-General: Sir Eric Drummond (Britain)

Main Bodies:

  • Assembly
  • Council
  • Secretariat
  • Permanent Court of Justice
  • International Labour Organisation (ILO)

International Court of Justice: The Hague

Failures of the League

  • No army
  • USA not a member
  • Germany resigned (1933)
  • Japan resigned (1933)
  • Italy resigned (1937)
  • Russia expelled (1939)
  • Dissolved in 1946

Treaty of Sèvres (1920)

Signed between Allies and Sultan of Turkey

Rejected by Mustafa Kemal Pasha

Replaced by Treaty of Lausanne (1923)

Important One-Line Facts

  • Immediate cause of WWI – Assassination at Sarajevo
  • Treaty signed in Hall of Mirrors – Versailles
  • Pravda means – Truth
  • League HQ – Geneva
  • Meiji Era – 1867–1912
  • League dissolved – 1946
  • Treaty with Turkey – Sèvres

Important Treaties

  • Versailles: Germany – Very harsh treaty
  • Saint-Germain: Austria – Forbade union with Germany
  • Trianon: Hungary – Independent Hungary recognised
  • Sèvres: Turkey – Rejected by Mustafa Kemal Pasha
  • Brest-Litovsk: Russia – Exit from war
